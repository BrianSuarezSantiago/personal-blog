One practical project I built using AWS came from a real problem at home. We had a large number of important documents scattered across different places, such as mortgage contracts and payment receipts, loan documents, home, car, and health insurance policies, appliance warranties, and utility bills for electricity, water, and internet. Whenever I needed to review one of these documents, I often did not remember where it was stored, in which folder or section it belonged, or which specific clause applied to my situation.

To solve this, I built a simple but effective personal document management solution using AWS. I used Amazon S3 as a single, centralized repository to store all documents, including PDFs and scanned images. On top of that, I created a knowledge base using Amazon Bedrock to index and extract information from these documents. I then built a Bedrock Agent that acts as a conversational interface, allowing me to ask questions in natural language and retrieve precise answers directly from my own documents.

Some real examples of questions I can now ask are when the dishwasher warranty expires, whether my home insurance covers a water pipe leak, how much I paid for electricity in November compared to October, or what the penalty is for canceling my internet contract. Instead of searching manually through folders or reading long contracts, I get clear and contextual answers in seconds.

The value of this project has been very tangible. It saves me a significant amount of time, reduces stress when dealing with administrative tasks, and centralizes personal knowledge that was previously fragmented. From a learning perspective, I gained hands-on experience designing a simple end-to-end architecture with AWS services, working with document indexing and retrieval, and building practical, real-world applications with generative AI that solve everyday problems.
